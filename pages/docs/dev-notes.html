Meta Description Added to All Pages
CRITICAL On-Page SEO CTR +20–35%
▾

What Changed

A meta description is the short summary paragraph that appears under your website title in Google search results. The current gsurf.in has none — so Google generates one automatically from random page text (usually from the modal popup HTML), which always looks unprofessional and irrelevant to users.

Why It Matters

SEOMeta description is a direct CTR ranking factor. Higher CTR = stronger behavioral signal = rankings boost over time.
AEOAI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) read meta description as the primary "what is this page about" signal when deciding whether to cite this page in answers.
GEOCity name in meta description reinforces geographic relevance for Bangalore/Whitefield local queries.
🔴 CRITICAL · CTR impact: ~20–35%

Structured Data (JSON-LD Schema) Added — Organization + LocalBusiness
CRITICAL Structured Data AI citation eligibility
▾

What Changed

Schema markup is code added to the HTML that tells search engines and AI tools exactly what type of business this is, where it operates, what it offers, and who to contact. Currently zero schema exists on gsurf.in — the site is completely invisible to AI tools.

Why It Matters

SEOSchema enables rich results — FAQ boxes, LocalBusiness cards, breadcrumbs. Rich results get 20–30% more clicks than plain blue links.
AEOThis is the #1 AEO fix. AI engines like Perplexity and ChatGPT use structured data to extract factual information for their answers. Without schema, GSurf cannot be cited by AI tools.
GEOLocalBusiness + ServiceArea + areaServed schema is how Google AI Overviews and Gemini determine which ISPs serve which neighborhoods.
🔴 CRITICAL · AI invisibility without this

Heading Hierarchy Fixed — One H1 per Page, Keyword-Rich
CRITICAL On-Page SEO Primary keyword signal
▾

What Changed

The current page has three H1 tags and none of them contain a searchable keyword. "Ultra Fast Internet! Better Service!!" has zero keyword value. "We Provide Unlimited Network For You" is marketing fluff. "Enterprise Broadband Plans" inside a div is structurally wrong. Google uses H1 as the strongest on-page content signal after the title tag. One keyword-rich H1 per page is a fundamental SEO rule.

Why It Matters

SEOH1 = primary topic declaration to Google. Must contain the target keyword. Multiple H1s signal low-quality content.
AEOAI tools use heading structure to understand page topic. A clear H1 helps AI extract the right entity and subject matter for citation.
GEOCity/area pages (HSR Layout, BTM Layout) must have location in H1: "Fiber Broadband in HSR Layout, Bangalore" = direct geo-signal.
🔴 CRITICAL · Core ranking signal

Multi-Page Architecture — 11 Separate Rankable URLs
CRITICAL Technical SEO Structural foundation
▾

What Changed

The entire website was one HTML file with anchor links (#home, #plans, #contact). Google ranks pages, not sections. The revamp creates 11 separate pages each with a unique URL, each targetable for different search queries. This is the most important structural change in the entire project.

Why It Matters

SEO11 pages = 11 independent ranking opportunities vs. 1. Each page can target a specific query: "fiber broadband plans Bangalore," "fiber internet HSR Layout," "business broadband Whitefield."
AEOAI tools cite specific URLs, not websites. A page dedicated to "GSurf Bangalore fiber plans" is cited by AI answering "best fiber broadband in Bangalore." A homepage section never is.
GEOArea pages (HSR Layout, BTM Layout) can rank for neighborhood-level queries with high conversion intent that a homepage can never compete for.
🔴 CRITICAL · Root structural fix

07
robots.txt + XML Sitemap Created
HIGH Technical SEO Index coverage
▾

What Changed

Both files returned 404 on the live site. robots.txt tells bots which pages to crawl (and skip). Without it, Google wastes crawl budget on modal HTML, form elements, and the portal.gaxiom.in redirect. sitemap.xml lists all 11 revamped pages — without it, deeper pages like /pages/hsr-layout/ may take weeks to be discovered.

Why It Matters

SEOSitemap ensures all 11 pages get crawled quickly after deployment. Submit to Google Search Console on launch day.
AEOBing's crawler (used by Perplexity and Copilot) respects robots.txt and uses sitemap.xml to discover pages. Pages not in sitemap = not indexed by AI tools.
GEOLocation pages (/pages/bangalore/, /pages/hsr-layout/) must appear in sitemap to rank for local searches.
🟠 HIGH · Required infrastructure

HIGH AEO / GEO AI citation + rich results

AEOThis is pure AEO. Perplexity, ChatGPT, Gemini all extract from FAQ-formatted content. A Q&A answering "How much does GSurf cost?" = GSurf gets cited in AI answers about pricing.
GEOLocation-specific FAQs ("Is GSurf in HSR Layout?") train AI on geographic coverage, making GSurf appear in neighborhood-specific AI answers.

BreadcrumbList Schema — City > Area Hierarchy in SERPs
HIGH Structured Data SERP real estate
▾

What Changed

With the multi-page hierarchy (Bangalore → HSR Layout, Bangalore → BTM Layout), BreadcrumbList schema enables the breadcrumb trail to appear directly in Google search results below the page URL — showing "GSurf › Bangalore › HSR Layout" before users even click. Required on all city and area drilldown pages.

Why It Matters

SEOBreadcrumb rich results increase SERP click-through rates by showing site structure. Users trust hierarchical results more than standalone URLs.
AEOBreadcrumb schema helps AI tools understand the content hierarchy — that HSR Layout is a sub-location of Bangalore — strengthening geographic entity relationships.
GEOThe Bangalore > HSR Layout relationship in schema teaches AI tools the geographic hierarchy used for local search and recommendation queries.
🟠 HIGH · SERP + hierarchy signals

AEOInternal links help AI crawlers discover all pages in a single crawl session. A well-linked site structure increases the chance all pages are indexed for AI citation.
GEOLinking Bangalore → HSR Layout → BTM Layout in footer reinforces the geographic hierarchy and ensures crawlers understand the location relationship between pages.
