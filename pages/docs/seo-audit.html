Critical Issues — 7 Found

01
No meta description — Google auto-generates random snippet
CRITICALOn-Page SEOCTR loss ~20–35%

The homepage has zero meta description. Search engines auto-generate one from random page text — usually from the modal popup HTML — which destroys click-through rates from search results. A well-crafted meta description with target keywords, city name, and a CTA directly improves CTR from the SERP.

SEO: Direct CTR ranking factor. Higher CTR = stronger behavioral signal = rankings boost. AEO: AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) read meta description as the primary entity-context signal when deciding whether to cite this page.
02
Zero structured data / Schema.org markup — invisible to all AI tools
CRITICALStructured Data0/100 Structured Data Score

No Organization, LocalBusiness, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, or Product schemas exist anywhere on gsurf.in. This is the most critical GEO/AEO failure. When someone asks Perplexity or ChatGPT "what are good fiber broadband providers in Bangalore?" — GSurf cannot be cited because AI tools have no structured, machine-readable data to extract. Schema markup is the foundation of AI citation eligibility.

AEO: This single fix has the highest probability of getting GSurf cited in AI-generated answers. GEO: LocalBusiness + ServiceArea + areaServed schema is how Google AI Overviews and Gemini determine which ISPs serve which neighborhoods.
03
Single-page architecture — cannot rank for individual service queries
CRITICALTechnical SEO1 URL vs 21 ranking opportunities

The entire website is one HTML file. Home, About, Services, Plans, and Contact are all anchor-linked sections (#home, #plans) on a single URL. Google ranks pages, not sections — so gsurf.in has exactly one URL competing for every possible query. This is the structural root cause of poor rankings. The revamp to 21 separate pages directly solves this.

SEO: 21 pages = 21 independent ranking opportunities. Each page targets specific queries: "fiber broadband plans Bangalore," "fiber internet HSR Layout," "home security broadband." GEO: Hyper-local pages (HSR Layout, BTM Layout) can rank for neighbourhood-level queries with extremely high conversion intent.
04
Weak title tag — missing city, product keyword, and correct brand casing
CRITICALOn-Page SEOPrimary ranking signal wasted

Current title: "Gsurf - High-Speed Internet Service Provider | Enterprise Broadband." Problems: (1) Brand misspelled "Gsurf" not "GSurf." (2) No city name — "Bangalore" and "Whitefield" are the #1 local modifiers users add to broadband searches. (3) Missing "fiber" and "FTTH." (4) "Enterprise Broadband" is misleading — plans start at ₹900/mo which is residential pricing. Title tag is Google's #1 on-page ranking signal.

Fix: "GSurf Fiber Broadband Bangalore | FTTH Plans from ₹900/month | Whitefield" — brand, product, city, and price anchor within 60 characters.
05
No canonical tag — PageRank split across HTTP/HTTPS/www/non-www variants
CRITICALTechnical SEOIndex dilution

Without a canonical tag, Google may index gsurf.in, www.gsurf.in, http://gsurf.in, and http://www.gsurf.in as separate pages, splitting the ranking power of any backlinks across all variants. Every page in the revamp needs a self-referencing canonical tag. This is required infrastructure for any production website.

Fix: Add <link rel="canonical" href="https://www.gsurf.in/"> in the <head> of every page. Each page's canonical must point to its own absolute URL.
06
Three H1 tags on same page — H1 contains no searchable keywords
CRITICALOn-Page SEOPrimary keyword signal wasted

The page has three H1 tags: "Ultra Fast Internet! Better Service!!" (hero), "We Provide Unlimited Network For You" (about), and "Enterprise Broadband Plans" (plans section). One page must have exactly one H1. "Ultra Fast Internet! Better Service!!" contains zero searchable keywords. Google uses H1 as the strongest on-page content signal after the title tag.

Fix: One H1 per page, keyword-rich. Hero H1 → "High-Speed Fiber Broadband in Bangalore & Whitefield." All section titles → H2. Sub-sections → H3.
07
NAP inconsistency — brand name appears 4 different ways on one page
CRITICALLocal SEOLocal Pack ineligibility

On a single page the business appears as: "Gsurf" (title tag), "GSurf" (body text), "Netrun Fibernet" (logo alt text), and "GAXIOM NETWORKING SOLUTIONS PRIVATE LIMITED" (footer). Google's Knowledge Graph builds entity profiles from consistent NAP signals. Inconsistency directly prevents Local Pack rankings and reduces AI citation confidence.

Rule: Brand = "GSurf" everywhere. Legal = "Gaxiom Networking Solutions Private Limited" in footer/schema. Never "Gsurf," "GAXIOM," or "Netrun Fibernet."

High Priority Issues — 8 Found

08
Logo alt text says "Netrun Fibernet" — wrong brand entity
HIGHOn-Page SEOE-E-A-T brand signal

The navbar logo has alt="Netrun Fibernet" and the about section image has alt="About Netrun." Google reads image alt text as an entity signal — if the logo says one brand and the page text says another, it creates an E-E-A-T penalty. AI tools cannot build a confident entity profile for a business with contradictory identity signals.

09
No robots.txt or XML sitemap — crawl budget wasted
HIGHTechnical SEOIndex coverage

Both files return 404 on gsurf.in. Without robots.txt, Google wastes crawl budget on modal HTML, form elements, and the portal.gaxiom.in redirect link. Without sitemap.xml, new pages from the revamp (especially deeper pages like /pages/hsr-layout/) may take weeks to be discovered and indexed. With 21 pages in the revamp, sitemap submission via Google Search Console is mandatory on launch day.

10
No Open Graph / Twitter Card meta tags — blank social previews
HIGHTechnical SEOSocial + AEO signals

When anyone shares gsurf.in on WhatsApp, Facebook, LinkedIn, or Twitter/X they get a completely blank preview — no image, no title, no description. This kills social sharing CTR. Additionally, Perplexity.ai, Bing AI, and ChatGPT's browsing mode read og:description and og:title as entity description sources. og:locale="en_IN" signals to AI tools that this is an India-specific resource.

11
All social media links are href="#" — dead links damaging brand entity
HIGHOn-Page SEOEntity association

Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, and YouTube icons all link to "#". Google uses social profile links as entity signals for the Knowledge Graph — they connect the website entity to social entities. Without live social links, GSurf's entity profile remains incomplete. Requires client to provide actual social profile URLs.

12
Emails hidden by Cloudflare — not crawlable by AI or Google
HIGHTechnical SEOContact data invisible

support@gaxiom.in and bd@gaxiom.in appear as [email protected] in the HTML source via Cloudflare's email obfuscation. While this protects from spam scrapers, it means AI crawlers and some search bots cannot extract the contact information. The fix is to add email addresses to the JSON-LD schema markup — schema is not obfuscated.

13
No FAQ content or AEO-optimized copy — zero AI citation probability
HIGHAEO / GEOAI citation probability: 0%

There is no FAQ section on any page. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) requires content structured as natural language Q&A that AI tools can extract. When someone asks ChatGPT "How much does GSurf broadband cost?" — there is nothing structured on the site to cite. FAQPage schema + visible Q&A content is the minimum required for AI citation eligibility.

SEO bonus: FAQ schema generates expandable FAQ rich results in Google SERPs, taking up 3× more space and getting 20–30% more clicks than plain blue links.
14
Plans mislabeled "Enterprise Broadband" — wrong intent targeting
HIGHContentResidential audience missed

Plans starting at ₹900/month for 50 Mbps are clearly residential pricing, but the section is labeled "Enterprise Broadband Plans." This creates intent mismatch — residential searchers looking for "home fiber broadband Bangalore" find enterprise copy and bounce. The revamp clearly separates Home Plans and Enterprise Plans with distinct dedicated pages.

15
No GeoCoordinates or ServiceArea schema — AI cannot determine coverage
HIGHLocal SEO / GEOAI local answer eligibility

No GeoCoordinates, ServiceArea, or areaServed schema exists. Google AI Overviews and Gemini determine which ISPs serve which neighbourhoods using structured geographic data. Without this schema, GSurf cannot appear in AI-generated answers to queries like "fiber broadband providers in HSR Layout" even if the company physically operates there.

Fix: Add areaServed with city names + GeoCoordinates of the office + ServiceArea schema listing all served pincodes (560066, 560076, 560102, etc.).

Medium Priority Issues — 3 Found

16
No BreadcrumbList schema — missing breadcrumb rich results
MEDIUMStructured DataSERP real estate

With the multi-page hierarchy (Bangalore → HSR Layout, Bangalore → BTM Layout), BreadcrumbList schema enables the breadcrumb trail to appear directly in Google search results under the page title. This increases SERP trust and CTR. Required on all city and area pages.

17
Core Web Vitals unknown — Bootstrap CDN causing LCP delay
MEDIUMTechnical SEORanking factor + UX

Bootstrap 5 CDN and Font Awesome 6 CDN are loaded synchronously in the <head>, which blocks rendering until both files download. This likely causes LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) to exceed the 2.5s threshold. Google uses Core Web Vitals (LCP, CLS, FID) as a ranking factor. A PageSpeed Insights audit should be run immediately after the revamp goes live.

18
No hreflang or geo-targeting configuration for India
MEDIUMTechnical SEOLocal intent signal

For a regional ISP targeting Indian cities, geo-targeting should be configured in Google Search Console (country targeting = India) and og:locale="en_IN" should be added to all pages. This strengthens local intent matching and helps Google understand this is a regional service, not a global one.
